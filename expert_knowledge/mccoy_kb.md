### The CRM Health Audit Framework
Client audit case study: Agency with 8,400 contacts, 4,200 were duplicates (50% of entire lead database).

Four categories of duplicate lead costs:
1. **Wasted ad spend**: Retargeting bloated audiences (paying 3x CPM for one person) -- $47,250/year for one client on retargeting alone
2. **Broken automation**: Duplicate contacts get multiple welcome/nurture sequences, leading to spam reports and deliverability collapse -- "Bob gets three welcome emails, three nurture sequences, three 'just following up' messages. He marks it as spam."
3. **Inflated reporting**: Strategic decisions made on data that's 50% fiction -- "Their monthly report showed 8,400 leads. The real number was 4,200."
4. **Missed follow-ups**: Real leads fall through cracks because CRM shows "already contacted" on duplicate record

Root causes of duplicates:
- Multiple lead sources (same person clicks Facebook ad Monday, Google ad Friday with slightly different info)
- Human inconsistency ("Robert on one form. Bob on the sales call notes. Bobby when his assistant booked the meeting.")
- Formatting variations ("555-123-4567 and 5551234567 are the same phone number")
- Business vs. personal contact info (same person, different emails)

Old solution: Hire a VA at $600-$900/month, 70% accuracy, and "by the time they finish cleaning it, 50 new duplicates have been created."
