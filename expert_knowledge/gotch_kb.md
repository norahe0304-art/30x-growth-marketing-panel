## 1. Search Everywhere Optimization -- The New SEO

### Core Redefinition

Gotch redefines SEO as **Search Everywhere Optimization** -- no longer just "search engine optimization." The discipline now spans traditional search, AI platforms, local pack, YouTube, social, and e-commerce search. Google alone has **five distinct products** requiring optimization: traditional search, AI Overviews, AI Mode, Gemini, and the local pack.

**"Don't get romantic about any given platform. You need to be platform agnostic."**

### The Two Games of Modern SEO

1. **Search Everywhere Optimization** -- be visible across all search surfaces and platforms
2. **Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)** -- influence the AI-generated answers themselves

### Acronym Landscape (Gotch's View)

- **SEO** = Search Everywhere Optimization (his preferred umbrella)
- **AEO** = Answer Engine Optimization (cleanest, most broadly applicable)
- **GEO** = Generative Engine Optimization (too confusing for most people)
- He is agnostic on acronyms but believes AEO is the best standalone term for influencing AI answers

### Two Mechanisms of AI Answers

| Mechanism | Description | How to Influence |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Static Corpus** | The LLM's training data with a cutoff date (GPT-5 cutoff ~Nov 2024). Information baked in during training cycles. | Broaden brand presence across the internet before training cutoff. Common Crawl indexes the web, so more brand mentions = more training data inclusion. |
| **Retrieval (RAG)** | Retrieval Augmented Generation -- the AI searches the web for current info when the static corpus is insufficient. | Rank well in traditional search (Google, Bing, Brave). Be present on retrieval source pages. This is where SEOs have the biggest advantage. |

### Retrieval Sources Hierarchy

**For the average ChatGPT user:**
- **Web search** (predominantly Bing for ChatGPT) -- the primary retrieval method
- **API partners** (Reddit direct API, news/media partners)
- **Direct fetch** (when a user pastes a URL into the prompt)

**For advanced ChatGPT users (1% of users):**
- User-provided data (uploaded files in ChatGPT projects) dominates
- Web search drops to maybe 15-20% of retrieval
- ChatGPT always checks uploaded docs before going to web search

**Key insight:** "Retrieval doesn't just mean search. RAG is a general concept for retrieving information from any database or existing source."

### Three Search Engines That Matter for Retrieval

1. **Google** -- feeds Google AI Overviews, AI Mode, and Gemini
2. **Bing** -- feeds ChatGPT and Perplexity
3. **Brave** -- evidence that Claude uses Brave for retrieval

### Platform Priority Map (Sun & Planets Analogy)

Google is the "sun" -- 90% of effort should go here. The planets:
- **Jupiter** = ChatGPT (50%+ of AI market, 700M+ weekly active users)
- **Saturn** = Perplexity
- **Earth** = Claude (Anthropic) -- growing very fast
- **Mars** = Grok (unique: uses X/Twitter for retrieval)
- **Venus** = Microsoft Copilot (largely driven by OpenAI)
- **Mercury** = Meta AI (integrating into Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp)
- **Neptune** = TikTok (huge with Gen Z for product/service discovery)
- **Pluto** = Brave (important as retrieval layer for Claude)

**"Google should be 90% of your effort. Don't get so wrapped up in the novelty of new platforms. Ground yourself in reality."**

### Five Google Products an SEO Must Understand

1. **Traditional search** (the index -- fuel for all AI products)
2. **Local pack** (unique ranking variables, driven by Google Business Profile)
3. **AI Overviews** (appears in traditional SERPs, different citations than AI Mode)
4. **AI Mode** (separate tab, more product/ecommerce focused, pulls from local pack for local queries)
5. **Gemini** (independent chat experience, slightly different retrieval sources)

### ChatGPT Atlas Assessment

When OpenAI launched the Atlas browser, Gotch found:
- **Home tab:** pulls from static corpus only (no retrieval). Not current information.
- **Search tab:** literally just Google Search without SERP features -- 10 blue links, using Google (not Bing)
- **Separate tabs** for images, videos, news
- Conclusion: no need to change SEO strategy. Ranking on Google remains the #1 way to influence AI.
- Atlas proves ChatGPT has essentially decided to work with Google rather than compete against it.

## 2. The #1 AI Ranking Factor: Brand Coverage on Retrieval Sources

### Core Finding

This is Gotch's most emphatic finding. Example: HubSpot shows 89% brand coverage across AI citation sources for "best CRMs" -- yet their own domain is rarely a direct citation. 72% of those mentions are unlinked.

**AI platforms do not discriminate between:**
- Followed vs. nofollowed links
- Sponsored vs. organic mentions
- Linked vs. unlinked brand mentions

If your brand appears on the pages the AI retrieves, you will be recommended.

### Brand Coverage Formula (Step by Step)

1. Pick 10+ commercial prompts using Rankability's free AI search query generator
2. Run each prompt across multiple AI platforms in separate threads
3. Extract citations from each answer (use bookmark-let tool)
4. Collect 50-1000 citation URLs into a Google Sheet
5. Exclude homepages and competitors; prioritize listicles, directories, media sites
6. Check: is your brand mentioned on those citation pages?
7. The gap = your prospect/outreach list
8. Execute: get brand mentions via sponsored posts, PR, partnerships, influencer mentions

### Nudie Jeans Case Study (Will Reynolds Example)

A brand doing poorly in traditional search but crushing AI answers:
- 2% traditional search visibility vs. 90% AI visibility
- Why: 40% brand coverage across 35 unique retrieval sources (14 of 35 mention them)
- Massive off-site marketing: 22,000 branded searches/month
- Tons of sponsored posts, PR, and influencer work
- DR 70 according to Ahrefs with powerful domains (including New York Times)
- **Lesson:** "AI search optimization is not just about your website. It's about total brand domination around your subject matter expertise."

### Competition Ladder for AI Visibility

| Competition Level | Primary Lever | Secondary Lever |
|-------------------|---------------|-----------------|
| High (e.g., CRMs, legal national) | Earned/paid media on 3rd-party sources | Owned assets (website, YouTube) |
| Medium | Balanced: earned media + owned assets | Traditional SEO |
| Low (e.g., local queries) | Equal weight: owned assets + 3rd-party | Google Business Profile |

### Four Metrics for Tracking AI SEO Performance

1. **Market-Weighted Coverage** -- is your brand mentioned in AI answers? Weight by platform importance
2. **Position** -- where in the answer are you? Top 3 = where trust lives
3. **Share of Voice** -- coverage + position + competitor density = one trackable number
4. **Brand Coverage in Citations** -- the leading indicator; percentage of retrieval sources mentioning your brand (linked + unlinked)

## 3. Link Building 2.0 -- From Links to Brand Mentions

### The Evolution

Traditional link building is evolving. The new priority order:

1. **Citation-source link building** -- identify retrieval sources from AI answers, get brand mentioned there
2. **Dream 100 outreach** -- the top 100 websites in your niche; treat as gold-tier prospects
3. **Traditional authority building** -- still needed to make your own site a retrieval source

### Backlink Quality Framework

Four dimensions of link quality:
1. **Relevance** -- two types: localized relevance (local softball league is good for local lawyer) and niche/topical relevance
2. **Quality** -- from a trusted, well-known entity. Look at the link profile OF the linking page, not just its DR
3. **Placement** -- in-body links > sidebar/footer links
4. **Anchor text** -- keep it natural; large majority should be branded/URL/naked/generic

### Anchor Text Distribution (Safe Zone)

- Branded + URL + naked + generic = ~97% of profile
- Keyword-rich = ~1-3% maximum for the whole domain
- Treat keyword-rich anchors as a "back pocket highest leverage move" used sparingly
- AI doesn't differentiate between follow/nofollow/sponsored -- but Google still does for traditional SEO

### Link Prospecting Techniques

- **Reverse engineer competitors**: Run top organic competitor through Ahrefs Link Intersect tool
- **ChatGPT Agent mode**: One-shot prompt for automated prospecting -- donations, guest posts, chamber of commerce links, local organizations. "Within about 4 minutes, we've got all kinds of link prospects."
- **Featured / HARO / Quoted**: PR-style expert quote opportunities; use knowledge base to respond on behalf of clients
- **YouTube influencer outreach**: Pay YouTubers for 1-3 minute brand mentions; AI extracts transcripts and measures sentiment. Cap outreach at 20/day (YouTube blocks excessive profile viewing). Use simple email template.
- **Sponsored posts on industry blogs**: AI doesn't discriminate between paid and organic mentions
- **Domain acquisition (Merger Technique)**: Find dormant but relevant sites with strong link profiles; 301 redirect to your domain. Example: gotchseo.com redirected to rankability.com. Use the "Warren Buffett punch card method" -- if you could only do 5 acquisitions ever, be deliberate.

### YouTuber Outreach ROI Calculation

- Get proposed pricing from each YouTuber
- Calculate based on views/subscriber ratio and brand mention integration pricing
- Prioritize by cost-effectiveness
- Go to channel > More > View email address to find contact

### Link Spam to Avoid

- PBNs and link networks
- Irrelevant link exchanges
- Aggressive keyword-rich anchor text patterns
- Expired domain abuse (buying expired domains purely for redirects)

## 4. Keyword Research & Content Planning

### Keyword Research Template Structure

Essential columns:
- Priority, Source, Cluster, Keyword/Topic
- SERP Features (for estimating organic CTR)
- Search Volume, KD (page-level link competition)
- CPC (indicator of keyword value -- higher CPC = more valuable keyword regardless of SEO competition)
- Current Position, Current URL
- Intent (informational vs. commercial -- kept intentionally broad)
- Opportunity Category

**"This template is one we've built many years ago and continually refined. You cannot do keyword research without having a good template."**

### Opportunity Categories

| Position Range | Category | Action |
|---------------|----------|--------|
| 2-15 | Low-hanging fruit | Optimize existing page, add links |
| 16-50 | Existing keyword | Investigate why underperforming |
| 50+ | Clustering opportunity | Check for intent mismatch; may need dedicated page |

### Four Keyword Research Techniques

1. **Google Ads Keyword Planner** -- direct search volume and trend data from Google. Note: "competition" column is for ads, NOT organic SEO
2. **Google Search Console** -- existing query data (clicks, impressions, CTR, position). Export up to 1,000 queries per URL. Good but missing competition data
3. **Google/Reddit/YouTube manual mining** -- autosuggest, People Also Ask, "Things to Know," subreddit topics, latest high-performing videos on competitor channels
4. **Rankability tool** -- integrates all sources: Google, GSC, AI-generated ideas, Reddit, YouTube, trending, knowledge base, e-commerce (Amazon/Walmart/eBay)

### Keyword Prioritization Scoring

The KO Score combines:
- **Domain-level competition** (competitor overall domain strength)
- **Page-level competition** (how many links needed on page level)
- Both are required together to truly know how competitive a SERP is
- Plus: current position, CPC value, relevance score, search volume
- Organic CTR potential factored in

**"Most tools only measure page-level links. You need both domain-level + page-level combined."**

### Zero-Volume Keywords Strategy

Most keywords are unknown to SEO tools. Zero volume does not mean zero value. Example: "AEO services" shows 0 volume locally but national trend is growing. Often the first mover wins because no one is targeting these.

**"Not a single competitor, not even one, is targeting this keyword right now. I could go and build an asset, boom, get it published, start ranking."**

### Cluster-First Thinking

- Pick one core topic. Go a mile deep before moving to the next cluster
- Build 5-10 supporting pages around one primary page
- Do NOT bounce between unrelated topics
- "This is a mistake I see all the time -- people bouncing from one topic to the next without fully covering that topic"

### Sprint-Based Keyword Execution from GSC

1. Extract commercial keywords from GSC first (bottom of funnel)
2. Use regex filters in GSC to isolate commercial intent queries
3. For each key page, export up to 1,000 queries from GSC
4. Categorize: variants (target on same page) vs. new dedicated pages
5. Test intent: search both variants in Google -- if traditional results differ significantly, build a dedicated page

### SERP Analysis for Keyword Selection

When analyzing a keyword in SERPs:
- What SERP features exist? (local pack, PAA, AI overviews)
- Which directories rank? (Yelp, Clutch = profile building opportunities)
- YouTube results? (opportunity to rank videos)
- AI Mode results? (may differ from traditional)
- Occupy multiple spots: local pack + traditional + directories + YouTube

## 5. On-Page SEO -- The 49-Point Checklist

### Technical Foundation (Points 1-8)

1. URL allowed in robots.txt for Googlebot, Bingbot, and AI crawlers (GPTBot, GoogleExtended, PerplexityBot)
2. Page is indexable (no noindex directives) -- check with Detailed Chrome extension
3. Page returns 200 status code
4. Page can be directly fetched by ChatGPT (test with thinking model)
5. Page indexed across Google, Bing, and Brave (site: search on each)
6. Page is HTML (not JavaScript-rendered) -- critical for LLM retrievability. "If your page is built in JavaScript, you're not going to be a good source of retrieval."
7. Core content is server-rendered and visible in page source (check for H1, H2, paragraph tags in source)
8. Self-referencing canonical URL set

### Intent Matching (Points 9-11)

9. Page matches dominant intent shown in live SERPs -- "Don't ever try to go against that"
10. Page includes elements needed to qualify for SERP features (e.g., local pack presence)
11. Content is current with visible publish/update dates. Check source code for outdated years (e.g., finding "2016" in source = bad signal). "LLMs tend to prefer fresh pages that have been updated very recently"

### Content Quality (Points 12-16)

12. TLDR/scannability -- LLMs extract chunks, so make content scannable. Clear above-the-fold messaging
13. Information gain compared to top results
14. Word count within **median** range of competitors -- lean toward low end. "Practice brevity. I would delete nearly 3,000 words. Focus on conversions."
15. Proper heading hierarchy: H1 > H2 > H3. Avoid being too H2-heavy
16. Reading level appropriate -- aim for high school or lower, not college level. "Lawyers always have really technical content but their clients are not technical"

### Keyword Placement (Points 17-23)

17. Primary keyword in URL (including city for local)
18. Primary keyword in title tag
19. Primary keyword in meta description
20. Primary keyword in H1
21. Primary keyword in first 100 words
22. Variation in H2 headings
23. **Cover ALL relevant topics/entities** (the most important content factor). Use content optimization tools to identify unused topics. "Even mentioning a topic once is sufficient."

### Technical Performance

24. Mobile page speed target: 70+ on PageSpeed Insights. Less than 3 seconds loading. "A 1-second delay can lead to a 7% decrease in conversions"
25. Mobile-friendly responsive design (condense browser window to test rough mobile view)
26. SSL certificate installed

### Goal Completions (Often Overlooked)

27. Phone numbers clickable (tel: links) -- make the phone number the "star of the show"
28. Forms tested and working (use Mailinator to test)
29. **Thank-you page** with expectation-setting video after form submission
30. Email confirmation sent after contact form submission
31. Strong calls-to-action throughout the page
32. Primary headline above the fold (UX 101)
33. Links should look like links (blue = universal link color; don't use blue for non-link headings)

### Conversion-Focused Design Principles

- Left-right format: headline on left, sub headline, CTA, form on right
- CTAs throughout the page for people at different stages of readiness
- Social proof prominently displayed
- "The number one objective should be how do we drive maximum goal completions, not how do we manipulate Google"
- Goal completions are "likely also a Google ranking factor"

## 6. Content Production System

### Six-Step Content Workflow

1. **Pick target keyword**
2. **Build knowledge base** -- brand info, USPs, testimonials, policies, sales call transcripts, NPS surveys
3. **Research competitors** -- across Google + AI platforms + Rankability. Create a competitor research report
4. **Create intelligent outline** -- using ChatGPT thinking model + competitor report in a ChatGPT project
5. **Generate first draft with AI** -- use service page mode for commercial pages. Takes ~5 minutes. Target word count based on median competitor range
6. **Human editorial process** (1-2 hours): simplify readability, verify facts, add brand voice, add proof

**"This is a draft. We'll spend an hour, maybe up to two hours, with a human going through reworking this."**

### The Content Quality Filter

**"If AI can easily create it, don't publish it. Add what AI can't fake: original data, real experience, real screenshots, real documentation, and real opinions."**

### Knowledge Base (The Lead Domino)

Every SEO campaign starts with building a knowledge base:
- Create a ChatGPT Project for the campaign
- Upload: brand basics, products/services, differentiators, social proof, policies/guarantees
- Advanced: sales call transcripts, NPS surveys, subject matter expert interview transcripts
- Test grounding: ask AI specific questions and verify it cites your uploaded documents
- "The AI won't make stuff up. It'll prioritize the documents you've given it."

### Anti-AI Content Strategy

Do NOT build generic how-to content on your website. Instead build:

1. **Experience-driven assets** -- building in public, behind-the-scenes, anecdotal stories
2. **H2H (Human-to-Human) content** -- interviews, conversations AI literally cannot produce
3. **Free tools/calculators** -- still a moat despite vibe coding lowering barriers
4. **First-party data** -- data AI doesn't have access to and must reference your brand to use. "Third-party data curation is diminishing in value because of deep research."

### Content Distribution by Platform

| Content Type | Where to Publish |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Commercial/transactional pages | Your website |
| Mid-funnel (listicles, comparisons, alternatives) | Your website |
| Informational (how-to, guides, checklists) | YouTube, LinkedIn, Reddit, TikTok -- NOT your website |
| Brand narrative (pricing, FAQ, comparisons) | Your website (control the narrative) |

**"If you're going to go after informational queries, do it on these other platforms. Don't do it on your website."**

### Competitor Seeding Technique

Create content that AI platforms love to cite for commercial decisions:
- **Listicles**: "Top 10 Best [Service] Companies in [City]"
- **Alternatives**: "Top 5 Best [Competitor] Alternatives in [City]"
- **Comparisons**: "[Competitor A] vs [Your Brand] in [City]"
- **Triple comparisons**: "[Competitor A] vs [Competitor B] vs [Your Brand]" -- ranks for both shorter and longer queries
- Repeat for every single competitor in your area
- These are often low competition topics but exactly what AI platforms pull

### Controlling the Brand Narrative

Critical website pages every brand needs:
- Transparent pricing (at minimum "starting packages start at X")
- Comprehensive FAQ
- Competitor comparison pages (you vs. competitors)
- Brand news/updates (positive sentiment content)
- "You don't want someone else answering questions about your brand"

## 7. Local SEO

### Local SEO Strategy Framework (30-Minute Build)

**Step 1: Benchmark Before Acting**
- Set up tracking across all search surfaces: local pack, traditional search, AI answers, AI citations
- Install Google Search Console, Google Analytics 4, and call tracking
- "Never take action before establishing a performance baseline"

**Step 2: Google Business Profile Optimization**

Critical fundamentals:
- **Correct city** -- biggest quick-win. Switching preferred location to match physical address can produce 5-10 position jumps. "I hate to use the word guarantee, but I've seen this so many times"
- **Correct primary category** -- match what top competitors use
- **Standard description** -- properly describe what the business does for entity optimization
- **Consistent NAP** (Name, Address, Phone) across all platforms
- **Review generation obsession** -- build a review culture; offer employee bonuses; create QR codes via Canva for review links

**Key insight:** Google Business Profile performance directly influences Google AI Mode and Google Gemini. Example: Roach Law ranks #1 in local pack AND top 3 in Google AI Mode.

**Keyword-rich business names:** Even a service area business with zero reviews ranked #7 in local pack because of keyword-rich business name. "It just has no business being here, no pun intended."

**Step 3: Citation Strategy (New Approach)**

Old way: build citations as a checklist, one-and-done.
New way: hyper-focus on 4-7 citations that actually appear in AI answers.

Process:
1. Run commercial queries in AI platforms
2. Extract citations
3. Identify which directories appear most (e.g., HealthGrades, Yelp, ADA.org for dentists; SuperLawyers, Avvo, Justia for lawyers; Angie, BBB, Thumbtack for home services)
4. **Do SEO on those profiles** -- optimize descriptions with relevant topics/entities using content optimization tools
5. Drive reviews to those platforms (80% Google, 20% critical secondary platforms)
6. Consider sponsored listings on key directories (AI doesn't care if it's sponsored)
7. Target word count for profile optimization: ~500 words (varies by platform restrictions)

**Step 4: Brand Consistency Audit**

Use ChatGPT 5.4 to scan the internet for inconsistencies. Especially important for businesses that changed ownership or rebranded. "5.4 uses web search anyway and does pretty much the same thing as deep research and takes way less time."

**Step 5: Technical SEO for Local**

- Remove overly aggressive title tags (no zip code stuffing)
- Write unique meta descriptions for every page (no boilerplate)
- Do NOT put phone numbers in title or meta descriptions (kills organic CTR)
- Avoid generic informational content ("How long do dental implants last?") -- AI can easily answer these
- Properly structured service pages: [Service] in [City, State]

**Step 6: Local Content Strategy**

- Service pages first (bottom of funnel): one per service per core city
- Build 25-50 service pages just from the broader category
- Then build localized content:
  - "[City] [Topic] Statistics and What They Mean"
  - "Most Significant [Topic] Cases in [City] in the Last 10 Years"
  - Statistics-driven linkbait focused on the local area
- Use ChatGPT prompts for local content ideas

**Step 7: Local Link Building**

- Sponsor/donate to local organizations (softball leagues, theaters, charities)
- Get mentions on local news
- Join local business associations, chambers of commerce
- Use ChatGPT Agent mode for one-shot prospecting prompts

### Doorway Page Warning

Do NOT create service pages that simply swap city names with duplicate content. Instead:
- Dominate your core city first with all service pages
- For adjacent cities, create listicle blog posts: "Top 10 Best [Service] in [Adjacent City]"
- Let AI create genuinely different content for each city variant

### Local Pack vs. AI Platform Disconnect

Dominating the local pack does NOT guarantee visibility in ChatGPT or other non-Google AI platforms. Example: Daiser Law Firm recommended by Google AI Mode but completely invisible in ChatGPT. The fix: build and optimize directory profiles.

**For non-Google AI platforms, directories are the primary influence.** When ChatGPT searches for a local query, it often goes directly to Angie, Yelp, BBB via direct retrieval (not search).

### AI Competitor Analysis for Local (7-Step Process)

1. Pick seed keyword (e.g., "personal injury lawyer Chesterfield Missouri")
2. Convert to natural language using AI search query generator
3. Run across all AI platforms (Google AI Mode, Gemini, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Grok, Claude)
4. Set up Google Sheet with Citations tab and Answers tab
5. Use bookmark-let to grab citation URLs from each platform
6. Extract brand names + positions from AI answers using AI tool
7. Identify top 3 AI competitors and top retrieval sources

**Finding from study:** 60% of retrieval sources came from actual competing businesses. All 5 law firms with best AI visibility were also ranking top 8 in Google traditional search.

## 9. Technical SEO Essentials

### The Four Pillars (90-95% of the Technical Equation)

1. **HTML** -- website must be HTML, not JavaScript. "If you don't do HTML, you are asking to be invisible in AI platforms." Be careful with no-code platforms like Lovable or Replit that may produce 100% JavaScript.
2. **Internal linking** -- "one of the most impactful things from a technical SEO perspective." The lead domino for crawlability, indexability, and retrievability. Every page ideally no more than 3 clicks deep.
3. **Crawlability, indexability, and retrievability** -- don't block AI bots, don't block search engine crawlers. Use Rankability's free AI search indexability checker.
4. **Loading speed** -- AI platforms are intolerant of slow-loading pages. Target <3 seconds. Aim for 70+ on PageSpeed Insights mobile (law of diminishing returns beyond that).

### Screaming Frog Workflow

- Run basic crawl on client site
- Integrate GSC and GA4 if access is available
- Check for aggressive title tags (zip code stuffing)
- Check for boilerplate meta descriptions
- Identify generic informational content that should be removed/relocated
- Export report and use for internal linking discovery (search title column for relevant pages)

## 11. SEO Predictions for 2026

1. **Organic CTR continues to decline** -- heading toward single digits for many query types. Average ~15% CTR on page 1 of Google. 60-70% zero-click searches.
2. **AI answers become the default entry point** -- Google has AI Overviews, AI Mode, and Web Guide. Interface shifting from link lists to AI-first.
3. **UGC is a massive LLM feeder** -- Reddit, Wikipedia, YouTube are among the most-cited domains. Google reduced unhelpful content by ~40% with helpful content updates.
4. **Standard SEO best practices still matter** -- strong technical foundation enables crawling, indexing, and retrieval
5. **Social platforms and YouTube dominate informational queries** -- shift informational content off your website. "For most informational keywords, it's just getting harder to justify blog posts."
6. **ChatGPT will add spam filtering** -- expect stricter quality controls on AI platforms. "Marketers ruin everything. Actually, SEOs ruin everything."
7. **Search everywhere optimization becomes the default** -- multi-channel, multi-platform, multi-product discipline. "Anyone who still thinks of SEO as search engine optimization in the classic sense is living in the past."

## 13. Key Gotch Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Search Everywhere Optimization** | Gotch's redefinition of SEO -- optimizing across all search surfaces and AI platforms |
| **Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)** | Optimizing to influence AI-generated answers specifically |
| **Static Corpus** | An LLM's pre-trained knowledge with a cutoff date |
| **Retrieval / RAG** | When AI searches the web to enhance answers beyond training data |
| **Dream 100** | The top 100 most important websites in your niche for outreach |
| **Competitor Seeding** | Creating listicle/comparison/alternative content that AI loves to cite |
| **Anti-AI Assets** | Content formats that AI cannot replicate (experience, H2H, first-party data, free tools) |
| **Knowledge Base** | Brand-specific document repository that grounds AI content in facts |
| **Citation Sources / Retrieval Sources** | Third-party pages the AI actually pulls from when generating answers |
| **Brand Coverage on Citations** | Percentage of retrieval sources where your brand is mentioned |
| **Market-Weighted Coverage** | AI visibility metric that weights mentions by platform importance |
| **Topic Domination** | Building deep coverage around one cluster before moving to the next |
| **Merger Technique** | Acquiring relevant domains/sites and 301 redirecting to your domain |
| **Clustering Opportunity** | Keywords ranking 50+ that may need a dedicated page due to intent mismatch |
| **H2H Content** | Human-to-Human content (interviews, conversations) that AI cannot produce |
| **Doorway Pages** | Spam technique of duplicating pages with only city name swapped (penalizable) |
| **Entity Optimization** | Ensuring AI can extract correct entity associations (people, places, organizations, things) |
| **KO Score** | Rankability's keyword opportunity score combining domain + page level competition, CPC, relevance, position |
| **Grounding** | When AI prioritizes uploaded documents over web search / hallucination |
| **Search OS** | Gotch's SOP and project management platform for SEO agencies |
| **Rankability** | Gotch's SEO software for keyword research, content optimization, and AI tracking |
| **OpenClaw** | Open-source AI agent Gotch uses for automated project management |
| **WhisperFlow** | Voice-to-text tool Gotch uses to 5x productivity |
