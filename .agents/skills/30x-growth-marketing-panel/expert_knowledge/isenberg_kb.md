## 1) AI Startup Ideas & Validation

### Core Philosophy

- **Distribution first, product second.** Smart builders start with distribution channels, not features. In the AI era, people who understand distribution are at the top of the hierarchy -- above product people and developers.
- **Subniche dominance over broad markets.** Do not compete in massive markets where the "venture boys" are playing. Find a subniche inside a big market (e.g., FIRE within finance for Gen Z) and become the default execution layer for that subniche.
- **Directories as the ideal learning vehicle.** Online directories teach you AI coding, SEO, lead generation, and sales simultaneously. They are "distribution-first" businesses. Successful directories help people save time, save money, or make money.

### The IdeaBrowser Framework

Greg built ideabrowser.com -- the "number one startup ideas and trends product on the planet." Core principle: validated startup ideas are generated daily, combining trending data with AI analysis.

- Use tools like IdeaBrowser to source ideas
- Feed ideas into Obsidian vaults for deeper exploration
- Use Claude Code to turn ideas into PRDs and then into code

### Idea Validation Signals

1. **Subreddit test:** Does an active subreddit or forum community exist around the problem?
2. **Search volume:** Are people Googling the problem?
3. **Willingness to pay:** Are people already paying for inferior solutions?
4. **Complaint density:** Use AI to scan Reddit, X (Twitter), and forums for recurring complaints in a niche
5. **Trend momentum:** Use tools like Last30Days skill to check if a topic has recent and growing traction

### AI-Native Business Ideas (Recurring Examples)

- **Online directories** (funeral homes, senior living, porta-potty suppliers, luxury restroom trailers, tap water quality)
- **Price monitoring tools** (sneaker resale prices, e-commerce price trackers using Firecrawl)
- **SEO gap finders** (identify keyword opportunities using web scraping)
- **Vertical SaaS with agent workflows** (niche-specific automation)
- **MCP servers as sales teams** ("massive opportunity to build MCP servers as your sales team")
- **Free tools as top-of-funnel** (vibe code a free tool, ship by lunch, it markets itself forever)
- **AI agent marketplaces and skills**

### The Paperclip Framework (Multi-Agent Entrepreneurship)

Paperclip is a system for running multiple AI agents as a "company":
- **CEO agent:** Use a frontier model (Claude, GPT) as your primary decision-maker
- **Specialist agents:** Assign cheaper models to specific tasks
- **Bring your own agent:** Use OpenRouter to access any model
- **Agent accountability:** Paperclip gives visibility into what each agent is doing (unlike raw OpenClaw which "falls apart after 2-3 weeks")
- **Model diversity:** Use different models for different personalities and strengths -- programmers and entrepreneurs use many different models

## 2) Community-Led Growth

### Core Principles

- **Community as moat.** "In a world where you can just copy other people's businesses really quickly, bringing in a community and making them feel like they're a part of building what you're building is going to be absolutely a huge moat."
- **The community could vote on what you're building.** Give users agency in product direction.
- **Celebrate community members who contribute.** Recognition drives engagement.
- **Hire operators from inside the niche** (Step 28 of the 30-Step Playbook). Your best hires already live in the community.

### Community Infrastructure

- **Discord as home base:** Post updates in community-formatted channels; merge pull requests from the community
- **Open source as community engine:** The thesis is that open source is ultimately more powerful because you have the backing of the community contributing to it
- **Turn power users into public case studies** (Step 27): By the time you hit growth phase, your most passionate users should be your marketing

### The Directory Community Model

Greg created a "free directory community" where members share techniques, templates, and results. Example: "This guy named Andy in our community built a tap water quality directory, no backlinks, started it back in November and he's getting over 40,000 monthly visitors."

## 3) Vibe Marketing & Brand Building

### What is Vibe Marketing?

"You can deploy complete marketing systems and campaigns right from the terminal, just how you would vibe code any product. That's the big unlock." Vibe marketing is the marketing equivalent of vibe coding -- using AI tools (especially Claude Code) to build and run entire marketing operations.

### The Larry Loop (OpenClaw Marketing Automation)

Greg's signature case study: a guy in a small town in England turned OpenClaw into a content creation marketing machine generating $300-400/month MRR automatically.

**The Larry System:**
1. Create an OpenClaw agent named "Larry" with one job: automate marketing
2. Give Larry access to TikTok posting and TikTok analytics
3. Larry creates content (images + text overlays) and posts as drafts
4. Human reviews drafts, adds sound (critical for algorithm), and publishes from mobile
5. TikTok analytics feed back into content creation (the "Larry Loop")
6. Iterate until you find winners, then scale to multiple accounts

**Critical TikTok posting rule:** Post as draft, then publish from mobile. TikTok penalizes content posted directly through API (assumes bot content). Draft posting also allows adding sound, which is a "huge boost to the algorithm."

### The Larry Funnel / Larry Loop

"The Larry funnel is the full loop of having your TikTok analytics, the content creation, feed the analytics back into the content creation until you get a winner, and then also your end goal." It is iteration-focused, not just content creation.

### 7 Distribution / Growth Strategies

1. **MCP Servers (Let AI sell for you):** Build MCP servers that return your product data so AI agents can discover and recommend you
2. **Programmatic SEO (10,000 pages in a weekend):** Use Claude Code to generate thousands of niche pages targeting long-tail keywords
3. **Free Tool as Top-of-Funnel ("The tool IS your marketing"):** Vibe code a free tool in a day, ship it by lunch, it markets itself forever
4. **Answer Engine Optimization (AEO):** Get cited by ChatGPT and Perplexity. Use FAQ format, schema markup, comparison tables that AI can parse. "If AEO in 2026 is where SEO was in 2010, first movers will own these niches for years."
5. **Shareable Outputs (The Spotify Wrapped Effect):** Make your product's outputs inherently shareable. Identify the output or milestone your user would screenshot and share.
6. **Reply Guy Strategy:** "Reply is the number one growth strategy. Multiple X power users all credit becoming a reply guy as the fastest path to growth."
7. **Paid Ads on Proven Organic Winners:** Run paid ads only on content that already works organically. "There's a good chance that if it works on organic, it will work on paid."

### Brand & Design Philosophy

- **Beautiful products win.** "Why beautiful matters is you want people to fall in love with your product."
- **Branding before building.** "The problem is what a lot of people do is they go back and forth with AI models when they need to kind of take a step back and then figure out the branding and then apply that branding to the app."
- **Use Weev.ai** for generating design assets -- create mood boards, generate multiple variations, save favorites, build design systems
- **Brand guidelines as prompts.** Brand guidelines are "a prompt in and of itself" that you bring into design tools
- **Anti-grid composition:** "Hero section use asymmetrical balance, headline left, product screenshots floating right at angle, sections below flow organic with varied spacing, not rigid 12-column uniformity"

### Content Creation Workflow (Kova Method)

1. **Analyze creators you admire** using Manis AI -- get style breakdowns, aesthetic keywords, typography systems, story structures
2. **Hero's Journey structure** for short-form video: Hook > Conflict > Build > Problem/Resolution > Tease/CTA
3. **Enhance static shots with AI:** Take a still frame from video, bring it into Freepik's Nana Banana Pro model, add objects/backgrounds to transform boring rooms into cinematic sets
4. **AI transitions:** Use start frame + end frame in video gen tools (Cance, Kling) to create impossible transition shots
5. **Prompting for video gen:** Always describe what the camera is doing first ("camera is static"), then narrate like a story. Use positive terminology (avoid "don't" or "not"). Use prompt editor to optimize prompts.
6. **Typography system:** Define three levels -- main title card, section headers, word-by-word captions
7. **Five-act structure:** Hook (visual first, verbal second) > Conflict/Stakes > The Build > Problem/Resolution > CTA

### Ad Creative Generation

- Generate 1,000+ ad variations at near-zero cost using AI image generation
- Two schools of thought:
  - (A) Find the winning angle/format first with simple ads, then polish with high-quality creative
  - (B) Start with beautiful creative to stop scrolling
- Greg recommends (A): "Identify those winners, then remix a thousand times"

## 4) Product Strategy & MVP

### Core Product Principles

- **Start with the simplest thing that works.** "Do you recommend for folks to just sort of get the MVP app out, play around with it on localhost, click some buttons, and then update with the visual design from there?"
- **Distribution determines product.** Choose your distribution channel first (ads, organic social, SEO), then build the product to fit that channel.
- **Design matters more than features.** Most vibe-coded apps "look kind of garbage." The unlock is applying proper design (via tools like Weev.ai) before shipping.

### The Vibe Coding + Design Workflow

1. **Generate app concept** using AI (Claude, ChatGPT)
2. **Research trending designs** using Last30Days skill -- "What webpage designs are getting the most love right now?"
3. **Create mood board** in Weev.ai with brand guidelines as input
4. **Generate visual assets** (buttons, icons, UI elements) using image models (Nana Banana Pro)
5. **Build design system** in Figma from generated assets
6. **Apply design to vibe-coded product** -- the branding layer transforms generic into lovable

### Product Design Principles (from Sarah's episode)

- Research trending designs before starting (use Reddit, Dribbble, design communities)
- Visual consistency matters more for product design than for people/photo generation -- "You just need colors, shadows, lighting to look the same"
- Isolate and reuse winning design elements across the product
- "Modern but not modern, analog but not completely analog" -- hybrid aesthetics stand out

### The Obsidian + Claude Code Productivity System

**Context is everything.** The quality of AI output depends on the quality of context you feed it.

**System architecture:**
- **Obsidian** as the vault (second brain) -- markdown files with bidirectional linking
- **Obsidian CLI** gives Claude Code access to both files AND inter-relationships between files
- **Claude Code** as the agent that reads, analyzes, and acts on vault data

**Key commands (created by Vin):**
- `/context` -- Load full context about life, work, current state
- `/today` -- Morning review pulling calendar, tasks, iMessages, past week of daily notes
- `/close-day` -- End of day processing, extract action items, check confidence markers
- `/ghost` -- Answer a question the way YOU would, building a voice profile from the vault
- `/challenge` -- Pressure test current beliefs using vault history
- `/emerge` -- Surface ideas the vault implies but never states
- `/drift` -- Compare stated intentions against actual behavior over 30-60 days
- `/ideas` -- 30-day vault scan with cross-domain pattern detection to generate ideas
- `/trace` -- Track how an idea evolved over time across the vault
- `/connect` -- Take two domains and connect them using the vault's link graph

**Core insight:** "I just focus on managing this vault. I just continually try and make it so that this vault has all of the information needed so that I can delegate to an agent. If it's not making the right decisions, I'm changing something on the vault, I'm not necessarily working with the agent specifically."

**Strict separation rule:** Vin does NOT let agents write into his Obsidian vault. "I always want it to pull from what I think about things, not what it thinks about things."

## 5) Revenue Models & Monetization

### The 30-Step SaaS Playbook

Greg's comprehensive framework for building software-as-a-service companies in the AI era:

**Phase 1: Foundation (Steps 1-9)**
1. Check new leads (website form, Google ads, Facebook messages)
2. Create content for your subniche
3. Build organic presence (Instagram, TikTok, X)
4. Use AI to research content ideas and create content calendars
5. Double down on organic angles that convert
6. Run paid ads on proven organic winners (Step 9)

**Phase 2: Systems (Steps 10-18)**
10. Capture emails from day one -- "Your email list is your foundation"
11. Manually perform the workflow (understand every step before automating)
12. Document every step precisely
13. Separate judgment from mechanical tasks
14. Turn mechanical tasks into agent workflows
15. Design agents to complete full tasks
16. Connect agents to real tools
17. Add orchestration, retries, and verifications
18. Store user preferences

**Phase 3: Scale (Steps 19-30)**
19. Launch narrow with high-touch onboarding
20. Publish measurable proof (revenue, hours saved)
21. Move pricing from per-seat to per-task
22. Shift to outcome pricing as value compounds
23. Continue adding workflows
24. Build brand trust
25. Orchestrate multiple agents across the lifecycle
26. Build switching costs through data and memory
27. Turn power users into public case studies
28. Hire operators from inside the niche
29. Reinvest profits into distribution and product depth
30. Become the default execution layer for that subniche

### Pricing Evolution Model

The SaaS pricing trajectory in the AI age:
1. **Per-seat pricing** (traditional, declining) -- "SaaS companies in the public markets are down 30-50% from all-time highs" because of this fear
2. **Per-task pricing** (current shift) -- Charge for what the agent actually does
3. **Outcome pricing** (future) -- Charge based on results delivered, not actions taken
4. **Value compounding** -- As you add more workflows and build brand trust, you can increase pricing

### Directory Monetization Models

- **Lead generation** (most common): Collect user information, pass leads to businesses for a fee (e.g., senior living directories collect a portion of first month's rent per referral)
- **Display advertising:** Simple but effective for high-traffic directories
- **Listing fees:** Charge businesses to be listed or to claim/upgrade their listing
- **Price transparency as value:** Bring pricing data to industries where it is hard to find
- **Data licensing:** The data itself becomes the moat
- Monetization "depends on the niche" -- there is no one-size-fits-all

### The "Picks and Shovels" Model

"If you understand how to get great data out of tools like Firecrawl and Exa, you can build the picks and shovels of the AI gold rush." Build the infrastructure and tools that AI-first businesses need:
- Price monitoring tools ($50-500/month per niche)
- SEO gap finders
- Data enrichment services
- Review monitoring and sentiment analysis
- Web scraping-as-a-service for specific verticals

## 6) Trend Spotting & Market Opportunities

### The Last30Days Methodology

Greg's signature tool/skill for Claude Code that uses trending data from X (Twitter) and Reddit as the starting point for AI-assisted research.

**How it works:**
1. Install the Last30Days skill in Claude Code
2. Type `/last30days [any topic]`
3. The skill searches X, Twitter, Reddit, and the web for things that happened in the last 30 days
4. Returns expert-level research on any topic -- from "highest performing cold email frameworks" to "most popular rap songs"
5. Use the research output as input for building products

**Key use cases:**
- Cold email frameworks: "I barely gave it any context and it had become expert in different cold emailing concepts -- the three Ps framework, ADA, intention-based data trigger framework"
- Competitive research: "Research Cloudbot from the last 30 days so I can build an enterprise competitor"
- Design trends: "What webpage designs are getting the most love right now?"
- Content ideas: "How to get X followers"
- Market analysis: Feed Last30Days research directly into Claude Code to start building

### The Compound Engineering Approach

Use multiple AI tools in sequence:
1. **Research phase:** Last30Days, Manis, Perplexity for trend data
2. **Plan phase:** Claude Code to turn research into PRD/plan
3. **Build phase:** Vibe code the product
4. **Distribute phase:** Larry Loop for content, programmatic SEO for organic

### Key Market Signals to Watch

- **"Sleeping on the web data layer":** Firecrawl, Exa, and web scraping infrastructure are underappreciated opportunities
- **Local SEO is still low-hanging fruit:** "Local SEO hasn't really changed that much" even as general SEO evolves with AI
- **Product-based niches are harder** than service/local niches for directories: "I don't choose product-based niches because it's way harder"
- **AI-generated content that looks human:** The gap between AI-generated and human-created content is closing. Creators who master AI tools have an unfair advantage.
- **Agent infrastructure:** "Everyone will basically have their own OpenClaw-like agents... it's a really big opportunity right now to get into that, experiment, and get ahead"
- **Skills as the new SaaS:** "If you are an expert in something, there's a unique opportunity to download your brain into a skill"

### The Promoter Concept

Greg's framework for podcast/content preparation using AI:
1. Record a 15-minute brain dump on your phone
2. Dump the transcript into Claude
3. Have Claude create ADHD-friendly overview (not a script)
4. Use Claude to research past episodes for what worked (views, comments)
5. Identify where in the script attention might drop
6. Create graphics to explain concepts

## Key Isenberg Terminology

- **Vibe coding:** Using AI tools to build software through natural language prompts rather than traditional programming
- **Vibe marketing:** Deploying complete marketing systems from the terminal using AI, analogous to vibe coding
- **The Larry Loop:** Iterative content creation cycle: create > post > analyze analytics > feed back into creation > repeat until winner found
- **Subniche:** A specific segment within a larger market where you can dominate without competing with venture-backed companies
- **Distribution first:** The philosophy that choosing and mastering a distribution channel should precede product development
- **Picks and shovels:** Building the infrastructure/tools that others need during a gold rush (web data layer, scraping tools, etc.)
- **The Larry Funnel:** Full automation loop from content creation to analytics to iteration to conversion
- **Outcome pricing:** Charging based on results delivered rather than per-seat or per-task
- **Always-on documentary:** Continuous narrativizing of a company's characters, pursuits, conflicts, and visions through documentary-style content
- **Agent-as-employee:** Treating AI agents like virtual assistants with specific job descriptions (e.g., "Larry, your goal is to automate my marketing")
- **Context vault:** An Obsidian vault structured to be the single source of truth that agents pull from for decision-making
- **Compound engineering:** Using multiple AI tools and skills in sequence to achieve progressively more complex outputs
- **Reply guy strategy:** Growing social following by becoming a prolific, valuable commenter on other people's content
