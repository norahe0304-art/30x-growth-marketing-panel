## 2) Seven Distribution / Growth Strategies

### Strategy 1: MCP Servers (Let AI Sell For You)
- Build MCP servers that return your product data so AI assistants discover and recommend you.
- "Building an MCP server in 2026 is like building for mobile in 2010."
- Zero CAC. The AI assistant becomes your sales team 24/7.
- How to start: (1) Identify what question your product answers, (2) Build an MCP server that returns that data (24hrs or less), (3) Publish to MCP registries (Smithery, MCPT, Open Tools), (4) Every AI assistant that connects now sells for you.
- Real example: fintech friend got 150+ installations in 30 days, $0 ad spend.

### Strategy 2: Programmatic SEO (10,000 Pages in a Weekend)
- Pick a keyword pattern (e.g., "best CRM for dentists", "roofing in Miami").
- Build data set using Firecrawl or existing databases.
- Create page template in Next.js / WordPress / Webflow.
- Use AI to generate unique content per page (not just variable swaps -- actual paragraphs).
- Math: 10,000 pages x 30 visits/month = 300,000 visitors. 2% conversion = 6,000 conversions. At $10 each = $60,000/month from pages built once.
- Start with 100 pages as MVP, monitor indexation, then scale.

### Strategy 3: Free Tool as Top-of-Funnel
- "The tool IS your marketing." Vibe code a free tool in a day, ship by lunch, it markets itself forever.
- Pattern: Build grader/analyzer/calculator > user gets instant value > user shares score > organic viral loop > upsell to paid product.
- Example: Ahrefs backlink checker gives a taste of the full product.
- Create a "free tool calendar" the way you create a content calendar.

### Strategy 4: Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)
- "If AEO in 2026 is where SEO was in 2010, first movers will own these niches for years."
- Get cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini. Use FAQ format, schema markup, comparison tables that AI can parse.
- Peter Levels: AI referrals jumped from 4% to 20% in one month.
- How to start: (1) Google the top 20 questions your customer asks, (2) Write definitive structured answers (clear, direct, citation-worthy), (3) Add schema markup and FAQ blocks, (4) Build domain authority, (5) Monitor citations using Otterly, Profound, or manual testing.

### Strategy 5: Shareable Outputs (The Spotify Wrapped Effect)
- Ask: "What does my user want to brag about?" Then make that thing beautiful and shareable.
- Examples: Spotify Wrapped (100M shares/year), GitHub contribution graph, Stripe Atlas incorporation milestone, Duolingo daily streak.
- Your logo should be subtle but present. It should feel like it is about the user, not you.
- Add share buttons that prefill the post with the artifact.
- Works in B2B too -- people share in Slack, Teams.

### Strategy 6: Reply Guy Strategy + Newsletter Acquisition
- "Reply is the number one growth strategy. Multiple X power users all credit becoming a reply guy as the fastest path to growth."
- 380 thoughtful replies, daily engagement, 40 before posting. Find larger accounts. Reply first consistently.
- Alternative: Acquire a niche newsletter. Buy a 10K subscriber newsletter for $5-20K. Inherit trust from day one. Search on Duuce.com, Newsletter Investor, or Substack.

### Strategy 7: AI Content Repurposing Engine
- One pillar piece of content (podcast, video, essay) becomes 50-75 pieces: 5-10 tweets, 3-5 LinkedIn posts, 2-3 short form videos (Remotion), 1 newsletter, 1 blog post, 5-10 quote graphics, email sequences.
- Use Claude Code, OpenClaw, Claude Dispatch to automate repurposing.
- "If you do this every week, in 3 months you'll have more content than your competitors."

## 3) The Larry Loop (OpenClaw Marketing Automation)

### Origin Story
Oliver Henry, a guy in a small town in England, turned OpenClaw into a content creation marketing machine generating $300-400/month MRR automatically without touching the apps.

### The Larry System
1. Create an OpenClaw agent named "Larry" with one job: automate TikTok marketing.
2. Give Larry access to TikTok posting and TikTok analytics.
3. Larry creates content (images + text overlays) and posts as drafts.
4. Human reviews drafts, adds sound (critical for algorithm), publishes from mobile.
5. TikTok analytics feed back into content creation (the "Larry Loop").
6. Iterate until you find winners, then scale to multiple accounts.

### Critical TikTok Rules
- **Post as draft, then publish from mobile.** TikTok penalizes content posted directly through API (assumes bot content).
- Draft posting allows adding sound, which is "a huge boost to the algorithm."
- Scale by running one TikTok per app, then scale each one.

### The Larry Funnel
"The full loop of having your TikTok analytics, the content creation, feed the analytics back into the content creation until you get a winner, and then also your end goal." Iteration-focused, not just content creation.

## 6) Online Directories: The Distribution-First Business

### Why Directories?
- "Directories are distribution-first businesses. They teach you AI coding, SEO, lead generation, and sales simultaneously."
- Successful directories help people save time, save money, or make money.
- $200-$500 to build. Can generate $2K-$10K/month passive revenue. 10-20 min/week maintenance.
- "If your timeline is under 6 months to make money, don't build a directory."

### Directory Case Studies
| Directory | Monthly Visitors | Revenue Model |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------|
| Parting.com (funeral homes) | 61K | Vertical SaaS + lead gen + ads |
| APlaceForMom.com (senior living) | 824K | Lead gen ($50M+/year estimated) |
| GasBuddy.com (gas prices) | 1.1M | Debit card ($10/mo) + ads |

### GasBuddy's Crowdsourcing Psychology
- Public leaderboard since 2004. Prince33 hasn't missed a day in 5,800+ days.
- Giveaways (e-bikes in 2004, $100 gas cards now). Points system: report gas prices = points = giveaway entries.
- "Everyone loves a public leaderboard. We all can't be Usain Bolt, but you can be the best gas price reporter."

### Frey's 7-Step Directory Building Process (with Claude Code)
1. **Scrape data**: OutScraper for Google Maps data (cheapest option). Got 71K rows for porta-potty suppliers.
2. **Clean obvious junk**: Claude Code removes listings with no name, permanently closed, big box retailers. 71K down to 20K.
3. **Deep web verification**: Install Crawl4AI (free, open-source). Claude Code + Crawl4AI crawl all 20K websites to verify niche relevance. 20K down to 725 luxury restroom trailers. Took 3 hours unattended.
4. **Enrich: inventory/products**: Crawl websites for product details (stall counts, types). Do one enrichment pass at a time, not a laundry list.
5. **Enrich: images**: Scrape images + use Claude Vision ($30 for 700 listings) to select best ones.
6. **Enrich: amenities/features**: Crawl for features that become directory filters.
7. **Enrich: service areas**: Crawl for geographic coverage.

### Directory Monetization Models
- **Lead generation** (most common): collect user info, pass to businesses.
- **Display advertising**: simple but effective for high-traffic.
- **Listing fees**: charge to claim/upgrade listings.
- **Vertical SaaS**: build software for the niche (e.g., Parting Pro for funeral homes).
- **Agency model**: website creation + marketing for listed businesses.
- **Affiliate**: Amazon affiliate for related products (e.g., water filters on tap water directory).
- **Price transparency as value**: bring pricing data to opaque industries.

### Directory + AI Search Future
- "In AI search, niche directories are the ones that stay. Horizontal directories get hurt."
- Directories serve the decision-making phase, not the discovery phase.
- "Local SEO hasn't really changed that much." Local queries still show organic listings.
- Build super-niche to play into the AI search era (3 links instead of 1,000 blue links).

## 8) The Obsidian + Claude Code System (Guest: Vin / Internet Vin)

### Core Insight
"I just focus on managing this vault. I continually try to make it so that this vault has all of the information needed so that I can delegate to an agent. If it's not making the right decisions, I'm changing something on the vault."

### System Architecture
- **Obsidian** as the vault (second brain) -- markdown files with bidirectional linking.
- **Obsidian CLI** gives Claude Code access to files AND inter-relationships between files.
- **Claude Code** as the agent that reads, analyzes, and acts on vault data.

### Key Commands (Created by Vin)
| Command | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| `/context` | Load full context about life, work, current state |
| `/today` | Morning review: calendar, tasks, iMessages, past week of daily notes |
| `/close-day` | End-of-day processing, extract action items, check confidence markers |
| `/ghost` | Answer a question the way YOU would, building voice profile from vault |
| `/challenge` | Pressure test current beliefs using vault history |
| `/emerge` | Surface ideas the vault implies but never states |
| `/drift` | Compare stated intentions vs. actual behavior over 30-60 days |
| `/ideas` | 30-day vault scan with cross-domain pattern detection |
| `/trace` | Track how an idea evolved over time across the vault |
| `/connect` | Take two domains and connect them using vault's link graph |

### Strict Separation Rule
Vin does NOT let agents write into his Obsidian vault. "I always want it to pull from what I think about things, not what it thinks about things." If agents start making their own files, pattern detection becomes unreliable.

### Greg's Takeaway
"If you are serious about using LLMs and you are not using a centralized note-taking tool that uses markdown as the foundation, you are not getting the most out of it. Markdown files are the oxygen of LLMs."

## 9) Paperclip Framework (Multi-Agent Orchestration)

### What is Paperclip?
Open-source multi-agent orchestration. You are the "board" -- you talk to the CEO agent, give high-level goals (MRR targets, what to achieve), CEO makes a plan and hires other agents.

### Key Concepts
- **Memento Man analogy**: AI agents wake up capable but with no memory. You must write context (like Memento's Polaroids) telling them who they are and what to do.
- **Heartbeat**: Every 30 minutes, the agent "wakes up" and follows a checklist (fetch identity, read today's plan, check assignments, extract memory).
- **Agent diversity**: Use different models for different roles. Claude Opus for comprehension/refactoring. GPT 5.3/Codex for fast iteration. Cursor coder and Claude coder as workhorse agents.
- **Skills marketplace**: skills.sh for finding skills. Check security badges and GitHub stars.
- **Routines**: template issues that rerun daily (e.g., "every day at 10am, read GitHub changes, post Discord update").
- **Shareable companies**: Import pre-built org structures (Gary Tan's GStack, agency agents repo with 60K stars, game studios).
- **Maximizer mode** (upcoming): CEO ensures someone is always working until task is 100% done.

## 12) Content Creation Workflow (Guest: Kova)

### Manis AI for Creator Analysis
- Upload or link a video. Manis watches it, runs scripts to parse video, transcribes, breaks down style.
- Output: aesthetic keywords, typography system (3 levels: titles, headers, captions), story structure, replication plan.
- "Find creators you like, get Manis to create a plan. It gives you ideas, vocabulary, and a mood board."

### The Five-Act Short-Form Structure
1. **Hook**: Visual first (show finished product), verbal second (state concept). No preamble.
2. **Conflict/Stakes**: Creator's personal challenge. Creates relatability.
3. **The Build**: Step-by-step progress. Circle numbers for steps.
4. **Problem/Resolution**: Classic dramatic arc (too slow, too fast, just right).
5. **Tease/CTA**: Seed the next episode. Creative CTA.

### AI-Enhanced Production
- **Static shot enhancement**: Take still frame from video > Freepik Nana Banana Pro > add objects/backgrounds (flowers, plushies, fairy lights). Transform bare rooms into cinematic sets.
- **AI transitions**: Take start frame + end frame > Cance/Kling video gen > create impossible transition shots.
- **Prompting for video gen**: (1) Always describe camera first ("camera is static"), (2) Narrate like a story, (3) Use positive terminology (avoid "don't"), (4) Use prompt editor to optimize.
- **Masking**: Overlay AI-generated elements on existing footage. Available in Premiere Pro, CapCut, DaVinci.
